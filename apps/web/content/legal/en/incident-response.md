# Incident Response Policy

Last Updated: 06.04.2026

This page describes how Proovra identifies, assesses, contains, investigates, and remediates security incidents affecting the Services.

## 1. PURPOSE

Proovra maintains incident response procedures intended to support service resilience, evidence integrity, customer communication, and compliance with applicable legal obligations.

## 2. INCIDENT LIFECYCLE

Our incident response process may include:

- detection and intake
- validation and triage
- containment
- technical investigation
- eradication and remediation
- service recovery
- post-incident review

## 3. PRIORITIZATION

Incidents may be prioritized based on factors such as:

- impact on confidentiality
- impact on integrity
- impact on availability
- impact on customer data
- impact on evidence verification capability
- legal or regulatory implications

## 4. CONTAINMENT AND REMEDIATION

Where appropriate, Proovra may:

- restrict affected systems or credentials
- rotate secrets or access controls
- isolate components
- deploy mitigations or fixes
- increase monitoring
- preserve logs and forensic records relevant to investigation

## 5. CUSTOMER AND REGULATORY NOTIFICATION

Where required by law or contract, Proovra may notify affected customers or authorities without undue delay after becoming aware of a qualifying incident.

## 6. EVIDENCE PRESERVATION

Where relevant, Proovra seeks to preserve security logs, audit records, and technical evidence reasonably necessary to investigate and document incidents.

## 7. CONTINUOUS IMPROVEMENT

Following significant incidents, Proovra may perform post-incident review and update controls, procedures, training, or documentation.
